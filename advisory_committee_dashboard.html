Key Findings
1Core curriculum (calculus, physics, chemistry, engineering mechanics) is strongly aligned with NJIT and Rutgers transfer requirements.
2NJIT Joint Admission Agreement is an underutilized differentiator — should be marketed more aggressively to prospective students.
3No articulation with Rowan University's Henry M. Rowan College of Engineering despite it being a top NJ engineering school.
4No Python or computational tools in current curriculum; Rutgers, NJIT, and most 4-year programs now require Python from semester 1.
5No Environmental Engineering pathway despite 7% BLS growth projection and strong NJ federal infrastructure investment.
6AI/ML tools and simulation software are absent from curriculum, increasingly expected by transfer institutions and employers.
7Electrical Engineering track has heaviest load (18 credit electives) with fall/spring-only constraints — creates cascade risk if a semester is missed.
8Students who don't transfer have limited intermediate credentials — a stackable certificate would serve this population.
